The New York Times Company Reports Consolidated Unaudited Earnings Results for First Quarter 2010; Provides Capital Expenditures Guidance for the Year 2010.
"The New York Times Company reported consolidated unaudited earnings results for first quarter 2010. For the quarter, the company reported that on a GAAP basis it had an operating profit of $52.7 compared with an operating loss of $61.7 million in the first quarter of 2009. On a GAAP basis, it had diluted earnings per share from continuing operations of $0.08 per share compared with a diluted loss per share of $0.52 in the first quarter of 2009. Total revenues decreased 3.2% to $587.9 million from $607.1 million. Net income attributable to common stockholders amounted to $12.8 million compared to Net loss attributable to common stockholders of $47.4 million for the same period last year. Income from continuing operations before income taxes was $41.2 million compared to loss from continuing operations of $75.4 million for the same period last year. Capital expenditures totaled approximately $4 million in the first quarter of 2010. For the year 2010, the company expects capital expenditures in the range of $45"
