Aes Reports First Quarter 2002 Results.
The AES Corp., the Arlington-based but internationally-focused independent power producer, announced that it lost $311 million (58 cents per share) in the first quarter after taking a $473 million charge to write down goodwill related to investments in South America. Excluding the accounting change, profits rose to $160 million from $111 million in the same period a year ago, a performance that chief executive Dennis W. Bakke said was better than the company had expected. Revenue rose to $2.72 billion from $2.49 billion.
